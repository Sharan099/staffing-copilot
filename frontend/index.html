How scoring & ranking work

Transparent breakdown of how candidates are scored and ordered for each staffing request.

Ranking (who appears first)

Candidates are sorted by total score (highest first). Ties break on earliest availability, then more years of experience, then employee ID.

Only the top 5 ranked candidates are shown. Partial skill matches are included in the pool — the scorer decides final order.

Total score formula

Each dimension produces a raw score 0–100. Your weight sliders set how much each dimension contributes. Weights auto-normalize to 100%.

Total = Σ (raw score × weight%) for each dimension

Scoring dimensions

Skills
Match % of required skills. Core skills (★) count 2×; nice-to-have skills count 1×. Adjacent skills give partial credit.
Availability
Can the person start by your “Available by” date? Earlier availability earns bonus points; late availability is penalized.
Experience
Years of experience scaled to 100 at 15+ years.
Location
100 if location matches your request; 0 if it does not. Ignored (100) when no location is specified.
Capacity
Bench employees score highest. Billable staff score lower as utilization increases.
German
Only for client-facing roles. Compares employee fluency vs your required level.

Weight slider labels

  • 0–15 — Nice to have
  • 16–30 — Somewhat important
  • 31–50 — Very important
  • 51+ — Top priority

Judgment flags & memory

After scoring, the system adds judgment flags (e.g. burnout risk) and staffing memory (prior approvals/rejections). These inform the explanation but do not change the numeric score.
